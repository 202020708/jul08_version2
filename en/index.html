2020/2021 New school year arrangement

To new students from the Hong Kong Institute of Vocational Education (Shatin):
In order to allow students to adapt to the new learning environment faster, please pay attention to the following arrangements for the new academic year 2020/2021:

Pay the first tuition fee (only for full-time new students)
Please pay the first period of tuition and other fees as early as August 14, 2020. If students fail to pay the required fees on time, their student status will be cancelled and the fees paid will not be refunded.

Course start date (tentative)
Full-time students in the new academic year will officially start classes on September 1, 2020 (Tuesday), and part-time students will officially start classes on September 7, 2020 (Monday).

Student card
The college will generally distribute student IDs to all new students who have completed the enrollment process for all freshmen through their department on the orientation day/first week of school.

Student Handbook
Students can log in to MyPortal (https://myportal.vtc.edu.hk/wps/portal) about one week before the semester or through the Intranet of the Hong Kong Institute of Vocational Education (Shatin) (http://stintra.vtc.edu.hk/) Consult the student handbook.


College Information
The college generally distributes information to students through MyPortal (https://myportal.vtc.edu.hk/wps/portal) and email. Students are kindly requested to browse the relevant webpages and check student emails regularly for the latest news.


Freshman Information Website
Freshmen can also visit the website (http://stsdo.vtc.edu.hk/admission/) to view freshman information, including:

  • Orientation Day for Freshmen (only for full-time new students): The website will upload the Orientation Day arrangement in mid-August

  • Class schedule: Students can log in to MyPortal (https://myportal.vtc.edu.hk/wps/portal) about one week before the semester starts. Evening part-time program New students can also check the class schedule through the Hong Kong Institute of Vocational Education (Shatin) website (http://st.vtc.edu.hk/esu/Timetable-c.htm).

  • Application for exemption unit/credit transfer: The application date for the exemption unit/credit transfer for the 2020/2021 academic year is the first two weeks of the first academic year/first semester. Application forms can be obtained from the one-stop student service center in Room 023

  • Financial aid
    Full-time students: Students studying for an advanced diploma can apply for financial assistance through the Student Financial Assistance Agency under the Government. Students who have taken the basic course diploma can apply to the Vocational Training Council through the Student Development Division for tuition fee waiver. For details, please visit http://stsdo.vtc.edu.hk/url/sfaa.pdf.

    Part-time students:
    Students may apply to the Student Financial Assistance Agency under the Government for an extended Non-Income Review Payment Scheme (ENLS). Some courses have been subsidized by Vplus Engineering Professionals (Enquiry: 2435 9423). Students need to pay attention to the eligibility for financial assistance and financial assistance programs. Students may not apply for more than one grant/subsidy program at the same time for the same course, unit or credit.
